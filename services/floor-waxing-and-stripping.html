Our Process

How We Strip & Wax Your Floors

Our proven five-step process delivers a durable, high-gloss finish that protects your commercial floors for months.

1. Old Finish Stripping

We apply a commercial-grade stripping solution and use floor machines to completely remove old wax, buildup, scuffs, and discoloration down to the bare tile.

2. Deep Floor Cleaning

After stripping, the floor is thoroughly cleaned to remove all residue, ensuring a perfectly clean surface for the new finish to bond to.

3. Surface Preparation

We inspect the floor for damage, ensure it is completely dry, and prepare the surface to accept multiple coats of wax for maximum adhesion and durability.

4. Multi-Coat Wax Application

We apply multiple thin, even coats of commercial-grade floor wax, allowing proper drying time between each coat for a deep, long-lasting gloss finish.

5. Final Buffing

After the final coat has cured, we buff the floor to a high-gloss shine, creating a smooth, reflective surface that looks and performs like new.

FAQ

Floor Waxing & Stripping Questions

Common questions about our commercial floor waxing and stripping services.

Most commercial VCT and vinyl floors benefit from a full strip and wax every 6 to 12 months, depending on foot traffic. High-traffic areas like retail stores and schools may need more frequent maintenance. We can recommend a schedule tailored to your facility.

We specialize in VCT (vinyl composition tile) and commercial vinyl flooring, which are the most common types found in retail, office, and institutional settings. We do not wax hardwood, laminate, or ceramic tile floors.

A full strip and wax typically takes 4 to 8 hours depending on the size of the area, the number of wax coats applied, and the condition of the existing finish. We apply multiple coats with drying time between each layer for a durable, glossy result.

After the final coat of wax is applied, floors typically need 30 to 60 minutes of drying time before light foot traffic. We recommend waiting 24 hours before moving heavy furniture or equipment back onto the surface for the best long-term results.
